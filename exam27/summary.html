1. Definitions

Simulation: A teaching tool that imitates real clinical situations in a safe environment.

Simulation-Based Learning (SBL): A teaching strategy that uses simulation to improve clinical skills, decision-making, and confidence.

Exam Focus: SBL bridges the gap between theory and practice.

2. Key Characteristics

  • Safe – no risk to real patients
  • Experiential – learning by doing
  • Reflective – includes debriefing
  • Realistic – similar to real clinical practice
Mnemonic: SERR

3. Types of Simulation

  • Low-fidelity: Role-play, simple models
  • Medium-fidelity: Mannequins with limited functions
  • High-fidelity: Advanced mannequins
  • Virtual simulation: Computer-based scenarios
  • Standardized patients: Trained actors
Mnemonic: LMHVS

4. Simulation Session Steps

  1. Pre-briefing
  2. Scenario execution
  3. Debriefing
  4. Evaluation
Mnemonic: PSDE
Exam Trap: Debriefing comes after the scenario.

5. Benefits of SBL

  • Skills improvement
  • Confidence
  • Safe error management
  • Teamwork
  • Critical thinking
Mnemonic: SCETC

6. Similarities & Differences

Simulation: Teaching tool
SBL: Teaching strategy
Debriefing: Reflection and discussion
Evaluation: Assessment and grading

7. Very Common Exam Points

  • Simulation is safe and realistic
  • SBL improves clinical performance
  • Debriefing enhances learning
  • High-fidelity simulation improves critical thinking
