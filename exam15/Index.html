Directing

Heart of management: instructing, guiding, motivating, supervising to achieve goals.

1
What is Directing? Definition

Meaning

  • Core management function alongside planning, organizing, staffing, controlling.
  • Instructing, guiding, communicating, inspiring to achieve objectives.
  • Superior-subordinate flow: starts at top, continues to frontline; continuous at all levels.
  • In nursing: creating work climate, motivating, supervising, scheduling, counseling, disciplining.

Forms

  • Oral directions.
  • Written directions: clear, auditable, simultaneous to all, reduces misunderstanding.
2
Why it Matters Importance

Essentials

  • Initiates action: employees cannot start without clear directions.
  • Coordinates many workers and tasks.
  • Motivates and builds belonging.
  • Helps adapt to change; maintains balance via promotion, rewards, cooperation.
  • Aligns staff to organizational goals; maximizes outcomes by using capabilities.
  • Keeps supervision, motivation, leadership, communication effective.
3
Principles to Remember Principles
  • Direct objectives: communicate and motivate for best performance.
  • Harmony of objectives: align personal and group interests.
  • Managerial communication: keep channels open; manager is key medium.
  • Comprehension: ensure receiver truly understands.
  • Follow through: monitor enforcement of policies and directions.
4
Techniques of Direction How

Consultative

  • Exec consults subordinates before deciding.
  • Pros: participation, better communication, low resistance, uses experience, better morale and coordination.
  • Cons: time consuming; may raise expectations; can trigger arguments.

Free-rein

  • Empowers subordinate initiative and solutions; often fastest results with skilled staff.

Automatic (Directive)

  • Manager gives clear, precise orders with detail on what and how.
5
Characteristics Nature
  • Pervasive: needed at all levels.
  • Continuous: daily across the org.
  • Human-focused: deals with complex, unpredictable behavior.
  • Creative: turns plans into performance.
  • Executive and delegated: every manager directs; conditioning behavior toward goals.
  • Top-down flow: starts at top, moves through hierarchy.
6
Elements (Key Actions) Execution

Giving orders

  • Clarity: clear, concise, enough info; explain reasons when possible.
  • Match to situation needs; tone and delivery matter for acceptance.

Supervision

  • Guiding and overseeing work and behavior to meet outputs.
  • Includes training, discipline, follow-up to ensure prompt, accurate completion.

Motivation, leadership, communication

  • Maintain effective channels; motivate toward goals; lead by example.
